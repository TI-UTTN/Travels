Las Vegas, Nevada

Las Vegas is the most exciting and entertaining city in the world. Nowhere else can you find a city that has all the travel amenities that only a complete resort destination can offer: luxurious casino hotels, fine dining, wonderful live entertainment and state-of-the art convention and trade show facilities. Las Vegas is a spectacular city, incomparable to any other. Everything that you would expect from a world-class metropolis, and more, is right here for your travel pleasure.

The Strip

In addition to the best hotels, there is always a unique atmosphere on the Strip. During most of the day, the hotels offer shows, among which the Bellagio Fountains stand out. On the Las Vegas Strip, in addition to the hotels and casinos, is one of the most visited shopping malls in the city: Fashion Show Mall. On the lower floor you will find clothing stores and on the upper floor restaurants and fast food chains. The best time to walk the Strip and enjoy its atmosphere is when Las Vegas dresses up: at nightfall.

Fremont Street

Until the creation of the big hotels and casinos on the Strip, Fremont Street was the most representative street of Las Vegas, where the most famous casinos such as the Golden Nugget were located and its lights appeared in dozens of movies. If your stay in Las Vegas is only two or three days, you probably won't have time to visit the old part of the city, but if it is longer, you should not miss a visit to Fremont Street. The best option is to go at night to enjoy the Fremont Street Experience.

The Hoover Dam

Construction of Hoover Dam began in 1931 and was completed in 1936, two years ahead of schedule and at a lower cost than planned. During its construction, previously unproven techniques were used. Today Hoover Dam supplies electricity to the states of Nevada, Arizona and southern California, and is visited by more than 1 million people annually. During a tour, the visitor center you will have access to different viewpoints from which you can admire the dam, Lake Mead and the Colorado River.
